Nairobi Spotlight

Discovering architectural landmarks inside the green city in the sun.

Colonial Heritage
Kipande House

A historic building built in 1913 featuring rough-hewn stone masonry and a clock face. It was used as a registration office where African labourers were forced to carry identification cards during British rule.

Modernism & Beyond
KICC (Convention Centre)

A monumental brutalist structure built in 1973. Features a unique circular conference hall resembling a traditional African shield and an amphitheater inspired by conical thatched roofs.

Colonial Heritage
McMillan Memorial Library

Built in 1931, this neoclassical library features towering granite columns and stone lions at the entrance. It remains a crucial repository of Nairobi's literary and colonial history.

Modernism & Beyond
Times Tower

A sleek modern office complex constructed in 2000. Standing at 140 meters, this skyscraper features a glass-and-concrete facade optimized for solar reflection.

Swahili / Arabic Influence
Nairobi Jamia Mosque

A gorgeous Islamic landmark in the CBD, featuring twin minarets, silver domes, and intricate wood carving details that mirror traditional Swahili coastal design sensibilities.

Modernism & Beyond
UNEP Gigiri Headquarters

Completed in 2011, this sustainable eco-modern office building is completely solar-powered and uses natural air circulation systems to run without air conditioning.
