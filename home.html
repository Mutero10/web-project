Architecture is the art, science, and practice of designing and building structures, such as homes, offices, and bridges It blends engineering with visual art, shaping the physical environment. Kenya exhibits a rich architectural heritage, from traditional Swahili coastal designs to colonial influences and modernist innovations.

Architectural Landscapes

A glance at the foundational styles shaping Kenya's structural landscape.

14th Century onward
Swahili Coastal

Exquisite hand-carved wooden doors, structural coral stone walls, and tranquil open-air courtyards built to catch the sea breeze.

Early 20th Century
Colonial Heritage

Characterized by dark stone masonry, deep wrap-around verandas, and classic corrugated iron roofing designed for tropical climates.

1970s - Present
Modernism & Beyond

From the bold, brutalist concrete curves of the iconic KICC to the hyper-modern glass geometric towers defining the Nairobi skyline.
